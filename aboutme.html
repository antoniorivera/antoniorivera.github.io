I am currently a junior at Massachusetts Institute of Technology pursuing a Bachelor's of Science degree in computer science and a Minor in Philosophy. Ever since I played my first video game (Pokemon Crystal) as a child, I've been amazed and inspired by what computers can do. This love of computers and video games led me to become interested in math, programming, algorithms, and theoretical computer science.

Most recently, I was employed as a software engineering intern at Ultimate Software for the summer, where I worked on both front-end and back-end development and writing unit tests. Before that, I was a grader at MIT for course 6.006: Introduction to Algorithms, and the semester before that I was a lab assistant for course 6.01: Introduction to Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. In high school, I tutored other students in math as part of the National Honor Society.

In my free time I enjoy playing video games, watching TV and movies, coding, and playing tennis.
